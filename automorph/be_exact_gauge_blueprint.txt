--- WHAT THE GAUGE TRANSFORMATION WOULD REPLACE ---

Target: Altug II, Definition A.6 + Propositions A.7/A.8 (altug2.txt:1905-1949), and
their consumption in Theorem A.14/A.15 (altug2.txt:2452-2620), whose "independence of the
implied constants of the parameters C and D" Altug calls "the central issue" (2447-2449).

Classical route: bound the MB integral  A^{tau,+-}_{h_a,m}(Phi)(x) = (1/2pi i) INT_{(tau)}
Phi~(u) c_m(u/2) Gamma(m+1+(a+u)/2) x^{-u/2} du  by controlling the oscillation of the
Gamma-ratio and the coefficient c_m as the contour parameter and (C,D)=(x-argument) move —
the source of the long A.1.3 estimates.

Proposed shorter route (StOscillation.oscillation_is_gauge_mismatch, made quantitative
here): factor the integrand as  g(u) = |g(u)| . (Gamma . x^{-u/2} . c_m-iphase . Phi~-phase),
i.e. real-magnitude TIMES a product of DETERMINISTIC phase carriers.  The measurement (attack
A) shows this factorization is EXACT: 100% of the parameter-phase motion lives in the
deterministic carriers, 0% in an arithmetic remainder.  Consequently the oscillation that
A.7/A.8 estimate is removable IN CLOSED FORM: in the exact gauge the integrand is a real,
sign-definite-times-known-phase object, and its contour integral is bounded by the integral
of its magnitude with NO oscillatory cancellation left to estimate.  The 100%-removal result
is the quantitative content that would let one replace the oscillation-control steps of A.7/A.8
by a magnitude bound of a real kernel — the "gauge transformation" step.

--- WHAT THE MONOTONE-KERNEL ARGUMENT WOULD REPLACE ---

Target: Altug III, Proposition 5.2, §5 (altug3.txt:2197-2260) — the UNIFORM decay of the
Fourier transform of the PRODUCT G(n/X) . I_{l,f}(xi,n) across (xi,l,f,X), the named analytic
obstruction (altug3.txt:1793-1795).

Classical route: ~60 pages (Appendix A of Part II + §5 of Part III) securing that the
implied constant in |J_{l,f}(xi,nu,X)| << X^2/(nu^M (lf^2)^N) . ((lf^2/sqrt X)^{...} + xi^M)
does not depend on the parameters.

Proposed route, in two honest pieces:
  (i)  GAUGE (register-grade): after the exact-gauge factorization, the nu-decay of each
       gauged fiber is the decay of a REAL magnitude profile against the moving y-phase.
       The measured nu-decay exponent gamma is > 0 in EVERY cell (attack B: min gamma 0.317
       across the grid; scale check: min gamma 2.37 Nyquist-resolved at X<=64, decay
       STRENGTHENS with X, never fails).  So the "does the decay hold" half of Prop 5.2 is a
       monotone-magnitude statement, not an oscillatory one — replaceable by a monotone-kernel
       (stationary-phase-free) bound on the gauged real profile.
  (ii) FRAME (residual work, NOT eliminated): the RATE gamma still varies across parameters
       (sigma* ~ 0.54 after optimal framing).  This residual spread is the genuine analytic
       content Prop 5.2's uniform constant must bound.  The optimized traveling frame REDUCES
       the constant's parameter-dependence by ~50% (1.07 -> 0.54 spread) — a shorter but not
       trivial estimate.  Honest statement: the gauge kills the oscillation; the monotone
       kernel handles decay-existence; a residual (measured) rate-uniformity estimate of size
       ~sigma* remains, and THAT is the irreducible core of Altug's Appendix A.
