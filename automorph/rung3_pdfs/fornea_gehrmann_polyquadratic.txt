                                              On the algebraicity of polyquadratic plectic points




arXiv:2203.15998v1 [math.NT] 30 Mar 2022
                                                                    Michele Fornea and Lennart Gehrmann

                                                                                          Abstract
                                                We establish direct evidence of the arithmetic significance of plectic Stark–Heegner
                                                points for elliptic curves of arbitrarily large rank. The main contribution is a proof of
                                                the algebraicity of plectic points associated to polyquadratic CM extensions of totally
                                                real number fields. Moreover, we relate the non-vanishing of plectic points to analytic
                                                and algebraic ranks of elliptic curves.

                                                                                      1. Introduction

                                           In previous work, plectic Stark–Heegner points were associated to quadratic extensions E/F of
                                           number fields and modular elliptic curves A/F under some technical assumptions. The construc-
                                           tion generalizes the description of classical Heegner points on Shimura curves admitting a p-adic
                                           uniformization, and combines Nekovář–Scholl’s plectic insights ([NS16], [Nek16]) with Darmon’s
                                           pioneering work on Stark-Heegner points [Dar01]. Moreover, Conjectures 1.3, 1.5 of [FG21] pre-
                                           dict the algebraicity of plectic Stark–Heegner points and their significance for elliptic curves of
                                           higher rank, for which some numerical evidence was provided in [FGM21]. The aim of this paper
                                           is to establish direct evidence for the aforementioned conjectures in the polyquadratic CM case.

1.2 The polyquadratic setup
For the rest of the introduction we suppose that the totally real number field F is a polyquadratic
extension of degree 2t of a number field F◦ , i.e., F/F◦ is a Galois extension with Galois group
G := Gal(F/F◦ ) ∼ = (Z/2Z)t . Further, we assume that E is the compositum of F with a quadratic
CM extension E◦ /F◦ and that the following technical assumptions are satisfied.
Assumption 1.5. We require that
   • every non-trivial subextension of F/F◦ is ramified,
   • all primes of F◦ that ramify in F split in E◦ ,

   • the elliptic curve A/F is the base change of a modular elliptic curve A◦ defined over F◦ ,

     whose conductor f◦ is unramified in F/F◦ ,
   • the set S consists of all primes of F lying above a single prime ℘ of F◦ , totally split in F .


Remark 1.6. The need for the first assumption is explained in Remark 4.2. The splitting in E◦
of the primes ramified in F/F◦ is necessary to construct Heegner points on twists of A◦/F by
characters of G, while the total splitting of the prime ℘ in F/F◦ is just a simplifying hypothesis
for the proof of Proposition 4.5.

Assumption 1.5 implies that the elliptic curve A/F is modular by quadratic base change for
Hilbert modular forms. Moreover, we deduce that the cardinality r = |S| equals 2t , and that the
prime ℘ is inert in E◦ /F◦ . By a small abuse of notation, we denote by ℘ the unique prime of
E◦ lying above ℘. Since ℘ is completely split in F/F◦ , the elliptic curve A◦/F◦ has multiplicative
reduction at ℘. Furthermore, if we set a℘ = 1 (resp. a℘ = −1) in case A◦/F◦ has split (resp. non-
split) multiplicative reduction, we have
