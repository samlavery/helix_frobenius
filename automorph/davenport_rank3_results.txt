RANK-3 DAVENPORT CONSTANT: COUNTEREXAMPLE HUNT + EXACT VALUES
Target conjecture (OPEN at rank 3): D(G) = M(G) for every rank-3 finite abelian G
  G = C_{n1} (+) C_{n2} (+) C_{n3},  1<n1|n2|n3
  d*(G) = (n1-1)+(n2-1)+(n3-1),   M(G) = D*(G) = 1 + d*(G)
  d(G)  = max length of a zero-sum-free (ZSF) sequence over G
  D(G)  = d(G) + 1  (Davenport constant)
  D(G)=M(G)  <=>  d(G)=d*(G).   A COUNTEREXAMPLE = a ZSF sequence of length d*(G)+1.

LITERATURE STATUS (verified, primary sources):
  - rank<=2 & all p-groups: D(G)=M(G) PROVEN (Olson/Kruyswijk).
  - rank>=4: D(G)>M(G) for infinitely many G (Geroldinger-Schneider 1992).
  - rank 3: EXISTENCE of a G with d(G)>d*(G) is OPEN. No counterexample known
    (Geroldinger-Liebmann-Philipp arXiv:1009.5835 p.3; Biswas-Mazumdar
    arXiv:2402.09999 (2024) l.52-53). Widely believed D(G)=M(G) holds.
  - Structural boundary (Gao-Geroldinger survey Thm 3.4): for G=C_2^r (+) C_n,
    n>=3 odd, d(G)=d*(G) <=> r<=4. So the "+C_2" lift first fails at rank 5,
    NOT rank 3 -> no rank-3 counterexample is manufacturable that way.
