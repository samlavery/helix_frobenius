PROBE 3 -- STRUCTURAL no-jump on the genus-0 base
==============================================================================
Schoen's cycle = the LOCUS in Sym^{2g'-2}(C') where AJ drops rank = special divisors
= |K_{C'}| = P^{g'-1}.  Existence of a PROPER such locus is a positive-genus fact
(Martens/Brill-Noether: special complete linear systems exist iff g' >= 2).

   g'   deg K = 2g'-2   dim Sym^{2g'-2}   |K_{C'}| = P^{g'-1}   proper jumping locus?
    0           -2            Sym^0        empty (deg<0)   NO (J=0, AJ constant)
    1            0            Sym^0             P^0 = pt   NO (deg K=0, |K|=1 pt = all of Sym^0)
    2            2            Sym^2                  P^1   YES (codim 1 in Sym^2)
    3            4            Sym^4                  P^2   YES (codim 2 in Sym^4)
    4            6            Sym^6                  P^3   YES (codim 3 in Sym^6)

For C: y^3 = f g^2 over P^1 the base is g'=0.  The mu_3-eigen-canonical bundles are
still nonzero -- W^{1,0} = H^0(P^1, O(d)) for a ramification-twisted d >= 0 -- so the
'ramification divisor as canonical class' idea gives a nonempty linear system.  BUT
on P^1 (Pic = Z) EVERY complete system |O(d)| = P^d = Sym^d(P^1) IN FULL: there is no
proper special sublocus to serve as the cycle.  The jump that IS Schoen's cycle needs
h^1(base) > 0.  This is the precise, field-independent reason the cycle has no genus-0
(hence no branched-P^1) analogue -- all three pillars (|K|!=0, J!=0, AJ jump) fail at
once, and the ramification twist repairs |L|!=0 but NOT the properness of the sublocus.
