## 0. The object, and the honest state of the art (2026-07-13 sweep)

**Abelian fourfold of Weil type** (Moonen–Zarhin). `A` of dim 4 with an imaginary
quadratic `K = Q(√−d) ⊂ End⁰(A)` such that `√−d` acts on the tangent space `H^{1,0}`
with eigenvalues `±√−d` each of multiplicity 2 — **signature (2,2)**. Then `A` carries
a distinguished **2-dimensional space `W_K(A)` of rational Hodge classes of type (2,2)**,
*exceptional*: not in the subalgebra of `H*(A,Q)` generated by divisor classes. Attached
invariant: a **discriminant** `δ ∈ Q*/Nm(K*)`.

Classification (Moonen–Zarhin): on a **simple** abelian fourfold the Hodge ring is
generated by divisor classes and Weil classes; and *if the Hodge conjecture holds for
abelian fourfolds of Weil type, it holds for all abelian fourfolds* (indeed all dim ≤ 5).

**CRITICAL RECALIBRATION — the fourfold case is now a THEOREM, not open.**
- **Markman (2019–2024): every abelian fourfold of Weil type (all `K`, all discriminants)
  has algebraic Weil classes** — "finally settles the Hodge conjecture in dimension 4."
  The proof is **transcendental / non-constructive** (hyper-Kähler geometry,
  hyperholomorphic + semiregular sheaves on generalized-Kummer varieties). It does **not**
  exhibit an explicit algebraic cycle for a given arithmetic fourfold.
- Earlier, with **explicit cycles** (Prym constructions): **Schoen** — `K=Q(√−3)` any `δ`,
  `K=Q(i)` `δ=1`; **van Geemen** — `K=Q(i)` `δ=1` (geometric).
- **Floccari–Fu (arXiv:2504.13607, 2025)**: new proof for `δ=1` and all powers.

So the honest register for the carrier campaign:
| register | status |
|---|---|
| algebraicity-open (dim 4) | **CLOSED** (Markman) |
| explicit-cycle-open (arithmetic certificate for a specific Weil fourfold) | **OPEN** except Schoen `Q(√−3)`/`Q(i)`-δ1 |
| genuinely conjecture-open | **abelian SIXFOLDS of Weil type**, signature (3,3) (McMullen curve, arXiv:2603.20268) |

The carrier's point-count detection of the (2,2) occupancy on a **simple** Weil fourfold
is therefore a genuine *numerical certificate of a class whose explicit cycle is unknown*
— not a proof of an open conjecture, but a computable witness where the transcendental
proof gives none. Frame it that way.

## 1. Verified obstruction (this scout's structural finding)

`weil_scout.py` PART 1 computes CM-type signatures **exactly** (holomorphic eigenspace
dimensions `d(n) = ⟨na/N⟩+⟨nb/N⟩+⟨nc/N⟩−1`, `c ≡ −(a+b)`) for the cyclic covers
`y^N = x^a (1−x)^b`, and enumerates all imaginary quadratic `K ⊂ Q(ζ_N)`.

**Finding (verified, exhaustive over ALL CM types, not just cover-realizable):**
for the degree-8 cyclotomic (abelian) CM fields `Q(ζ_N)`, `N ∈ {15,16,20,24}`:
- SIMPLE (primitive CM type) fourfolds are **all signature (1,3)/(3,1)** — and
  measured **excess 0** (nondegenerate: only the 6 divisor Tate classes, no exceptional
  classes at all);
- **balanced (2,2) [Weil type]** CM types are **all imprimitive** → **non-simple**;
- **# primitive-AND-balanced-(2,2) = 0** for every one of `N=15,16,20,24`.

Consequence: **no simple abelian fourfold of Weil type has cyclotomic (abelian) CM.**
A *simple* balanced-(2,2) Weil fourfold requires a **non-abelian degree-8 CM field**
— exactly Mumford's 1969 construction (the polarization twist of Shioda's fourfold).
This is *why* the easy point-countable route (cyclic covers / Jacobi sums) cannot hand
you a simple open-flavor target directly; it explains Mumford's twist as a necessity.

## 3. Counting recipes (the data shapes for the detector)

All produce the **degree-8 Frobenius L-polynomial** `P_p(T)=∏_{i=1}^{8}(1−α_i T)`,
`|α_i|=√p`, palindromic Weil polynomial with `P_p(T)=p^4T^8 P_p(1/(pT))`. From it the
detector reads `H^4=∧^4H^1`: the reciprocal roots on `H^4` are the 70 four-fold products
`α_i α_j α_k α_l`; **#Tate classes = #{these = p²}**, and the exceptional occupancy is
that count minus the 6-class divisor baseline (two conjugate-pair products `α_nα_{−n}=p`).

**Recipe I — Jacobi sums (rungs B, C; CM fourfolds), `p ≡ 1 mod N`:**
- primitive root `g`, index table `ind`, character `χ(t)=ζ_N^{ind(t)}` of order `N`;
- for each `n ∈ (Z/N)*`: reciprocal root `α_n = J(χ^{na}, χ^{nb})`,
  `J(χ^i,χ^j)=Σ_{t≠0,1} χ^i(t)χ^j(1−t)` (accumulate exact integer coeff vector over `ζ_N`);
- `P_p(T)=∏_{n∈(Z/N)*}(1−α_n T)`.
- **Validated oracle-free** (`weil_scout.py` PART 2, ALL PASS):
  purity `|J|²=p` (Weil bound, rel ~1e-15); integer `P_p` (rel ~1e-15); functional
  equation; and **brute affine-count reconstruction** `#{y^N=x^a(1−x)^b} = p + Σ_{j=1}^{N-1} J_j`.

**Recipe II — CM-agnostic point counts (rungs D, F, G; any genus-4 curve), any good `p`:**
- `s_k = p^k + 1 − #C(F_{p^k})` for `k=1..4` (power sums of the 8 `α_i`);
- Newton's identities → `e_1..e_4` → `P_p` (functional equation fixes `c_5..c_8`).
- **Validated non-circularly** (`weil_scout.py` PART 4): for `C:y²=x⁹−1`, the `P_p` built
  from `k=1..4` **predicts `#C(F_{p^5})` exactly** (`p=5`: 3126/3126; `p=7`: 17044/17044),
  and all 8 roots lie on `|α|=√p`. This path needs **no CM structure** → it is the recipe
  for the non-CM / non-abelian-CM simple targets (E via its CM data, F, G).

**Recipe III — product (rung A):** `P_p(T)=(1−a_pT+pT²)^4` from `a_p(E)`. `#Tate=36` at
ordinary `p` (the `C(4,2)²` conjugate-pairing count; all divisor/correspondence, algebraic).

**6.1 The K-eigen rails are native.** `H¹⊗ℂ = W ⊕ W̄`, the σ/σ̄ eigenspaces of `K=Q(√−d)`.
For a cyclic cover of degree `n=|K-torsion|` (here `n=3` for `Q(√−3)`), `W`/`W̄` = the

`W = {n ≡ 1 mod 3}`, `W̄ = {n ≡ 2 mod 3}` (for `Q(√−3)`). Jacobi sums ARE the K-Hecke
character values, so the recipe delivers the rail labeling exactly.

**6.2 The rail-FREEZE observable (the detector's operational reading), validated.**
The Weil class is `∧^{g/2}W ⊕ ∧^{g/2}W̄` (type (g/2,g/2)). Its Frobenius scalar
`∧^{g/2}W = ∏_{n∈W-rail} J_n`. Reading `∧^{g/2}W / p^{g/2}`:
- `= 1` exactly ⟺ the Weil class FREEZES (Tate class over `F_p`, trivial Hecke character);
- `= p^{g/2}·ω`, `ω` a root of unity ⟺ freezes modulo a finite-order Hecke character of
  order = ord(ω); the CHARACTER ORDER is the discriminant regime parameter.
- non-finite-order phase ⟺ no Weil class.

**Why 12? — the orbit-count structure (and where it stops).** The balanced-`(n,n)` CM types
of a degree-`4n` abelian CM field over an imaginary quadratic `K` number **exactly `C(2n,n)`**
(combinatorial: the `2n` conjugate pairs each split `1`–`1` across `K`'s two cosets, pick `n`
to be on the `σ`-side). primitive = balanced − imprimitive. Measured:
`n=2` (dim 4): `C(4,2)=6` balanced, `6` imprimitive, **`0` primitive** (the obstruction);
`n=3` (dim 6): `C(6,3)=20` balanced, `8` imprimitive, **`12` primitive — UNIFORM** across every
degree-12 abelian CM field (cyclotomic + composite). BUT the uniformity is **dim-6-special,
not a universal law**: at `n=4` (dim 8) the primitive-balanced count is FIELD-DEPENDENT —
`48` for `Q(ζ₃₂)` vs `32` for `Q(ζ₄₈)`, `Q(ζ₆₀)` (all `φ=16`). So the team-lead's "count
depends only on the degree" conjecture holds at dim 6 (the uniform 12 is genuine and
striking) but **breaks at dim 8** — the imprimitive/induction count picks up field-dependence
once the intermediate-CM-subfield lattice is rich enough. Honest register: `balanced=C(2n,n)`
is a theorem-shaped combinatorial identity; the primitive count is uniform only at `n≤3`.

| 4 | `Q(i)` | 4 | 6 (primitive-μ₄ part) | (3,3), **disc 1** | Koike — **sixfold** |

The Weil-Hodge space is `U_Weil = ∧^{2g(C')−2}_{Q[G]_{nt}} H¹(B,Q)`, dim `|G|−1`, and Schoen's
cycles (via `Sym^{2g'−2}(C')` + Abel–Jacobi + geometric class-field theory, Patel–Zhang)

## 9. THE FREEZE-ORDER LAW (pre-registered P2 confirmed) — the disc regime, analytically

> **Freeze-order law:** `ord(r) = ord χ₃,p(D)`, `D = disc(f)·disc(g)²` (`f` = exponent-1
> factor, `g` = exponent-2 factor). The Weil class **freezes (order 1, Tate over `F_p`) ⟺
